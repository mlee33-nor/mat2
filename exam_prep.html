Skill

Solving with Common Bases

Use the one-to-one property: rewrite both sides as a power of the same base, then set exponents equal. Essential skill bridging 6.1 → 6.3.

Strategy

  1. Identify the smallest base that divides both sides. e.g. for $8^x=16$, both are powers of 2.
  2. Rewrite: $8=2^3$, $16=2^4$, so $2^{3x}=2^4$.
  3. One-to-one property: $3x=4 \Rightarrow x=\tfrac{4}{3}$.
Common substitutions:
  • $4=2^2$, $8=2^3$, $16=2^4$, $32=2^5$, $64=2^6$
  • $9=3^2$, $27=3^3$, $81=3^4$
  • $25=5^2$, $125=5^3$
  • $\tfrac{1}{b^n}=b^{-n}$ · $\sqrt{b}=b^{1/2}$ · $\sqrt[3]{b}=b^{1/3}$

Cheat Sheet

Everything from 6.1–6.4 on one page. Print-friendly.

Exponential $f(x)=b^x$ · 6.1

  • Need $b>0$, $b\ne1$. Domain $(-\infty,\infty)$, Range $(0,\infty)$.
  • Asymptote $y=0$. y-intercept $(0,1)$. No x-intercept.
  • $b>1$ grows (increasing). $0
  • One-to-one: $b^a=b^c\Rightarrow a=c$.

Transforms & $e$ · 6.2

  • $b^{x+c}$ shift left $c$. $b^{x-c}$ shift right $c$.
  • $b^x+k$ shift up $k$ — asymptote becomes $y=k$.
  • $-b^x$ reflect over x-axis. $b^{-x}$ reflect over y-axis.
  • $e\approx 2.718$; $e^x$ grows (between $2^x$ and $3^x$).

⚠ Sign traps

  • $b^{-x+1}=b^{-(x-1)}$ → reflect y-axis then shift right 1 (not left).
  • $b^{1-x}=b^{-(x-1)}$ — same as above.
  • $b^{-x-2}=b^{-(x+2)}$ → reflect y-axis, shift left 2.
  • $5-2e^{x+1}$ → reflect x-axis, stretch ×2, left 1, up 5. Asymptote $y=5$, range $(-\infty,5)$.
  • $a-b^x$ = $-b^x+a$: reflect x-axis, asymptote $y=a$, range $(-\infty,a)$.
  • Rule: factor the coefficient of $x$ out of the exponent before reading the shift. The sign of the shift flips when $m<0$.

Logs · 6.3

$x=b^y \;\Longleftrightarrow\; y=\log_b x$
  • $\log x=\log_{10}x$ · $\ln x=\log_e x$
  • $\log_b b^x=x$ · $b^{\log_b x}=x$

Log graphs · 6.4

  • Parent $y=\log_b x$: Domain $(0,\infty)$, Range $(-\infty,\infty)$, VA $x=0$, x-int $(1,0)$.
  • $y=\log_b(\text{arg})$: set argument $>0$ for domain, $=0$ for VA, $=1$ for x-int.
